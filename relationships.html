Ian Barbour's 4 Relationships Model

Which one best describes the relationship between science and religion?

Conflict

Independence

Which one best describes the relationship between science and religion?

Conflict

This model portrays religion and science as competitive ideologies at odds. They are never to be intertwined or discussed as relating to one another. Religion, referring to the existence of God and his laws is spiritual and metaphysical, therefore, it cannot be related to science which has been discovered by humans on Earth. Some people have strong faith in their religion while others have faith in science as if it was their spiritual belief. This makes both ideas contradict.

Independence

Like conflict, science and religion should never be contemplated as being relevant to one another. They should remain separate as they provide different answers. Religion provides answers regarding human ethics, emotions and views of afterlife. Science provides answers for everyday natural occurrences such as gravity or medicine.

Integration

This model intertwines science and religion, looking to combine details of both ideologies. This can only be done in certain cases when using science in order to do good in the world such as taking care of the environment. However, science and religion can contradict each other depending on the specific religion and the science provided. In order to contemplate integrating the two concepts, one must reject particular details on both sides.

Dialogue

This model is unique in that it allows for the discussion of contemplating the ideas of science and religion without fully combining or separating the two. Since science and religion do provide different answers for different questions. We can use those answers to examine various situations. For example, Pope Francis proposes this model in order to bring attention to the ecological crisis present in the world today. He states that science has provided concrete proof of the degradation of the environment, referring to the high levels of pollution, the water crisis and evidence of elevated carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere. Using this information along with the biblical scripture which states that humans must be environmental stewards and take care of the Earth, Francis proposes a thoughtful case on viewing the benefits of science and religion.
